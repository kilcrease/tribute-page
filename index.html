Here's a time line of Kobe Bryant's illustrious career

  • (1996) Drafted and traded - With the 13th pick in 1996 draft, the Charlotte Hormets selected Kobe Bryant who later traded him to the Los Angeles Lakers for Vlade Divok. At the age of 17, Kobe was only a minor so his parents had to cosign his 3-year 3.5 million dollar contract.
  • (1997) Slam Dunk Contest Champion - Bryant did not make the All-Star team in his first year, but he did participate in the Rookie Challenge, and he won the Slam Dunk Contest. He barely made it out of the first round only scoring a 37, but went on to clinch the title with a 49 in the final round.
  • (1999-2002) Phil Jackson arrives and the three-peat - Phil Jackson arrived to the Lakers from the Bulls and the Lakers went on to win titles in 2000, 2001, and 2002. To this day no other team has won three straight titles.
  • (2004) Fallout with Shaq - Bryant and O'Neal had butted heads since they first teamed up in 1996. They stuck it out until a bad outing in the 2004 NBA Final against the Detroit Pistons. O'Neal demanded a trade and was sent to the Miami Heat. Kobe signed a 7-year $136 million contract the next day.
  • (2005) Phil Jackson returns - The Lakers hired Rudy Tomjanovich to repalce Jackson as head coach. He signed a five-year deal, but wouldn't stay in the role long He resigned due to poor health after just 41 games. Phil returned the following year.
  • (2006) Career-high 81 points - On Jan. 22, 2006, Bryant scored a career-high 81 points against the Toronto Raptors.. the second highest point total in a single game behind Wilt Chamberlain's 100 point game in 1962.
  • (2008) Olympian - Bryant joined team USA ahead of the 2008 summer games in Beijing. In eight games, Bryant averaged 15 points while leading the team to a gold medal over Spain.
  • (2008-2010) Return to championship form - Having never won a ring without Shaq the new dynamic in Los Angels faced a hard road. In 2008 the Laker traded for Pau Gasol and made it to the finals where they fell short to the Boston Celtics. The next year the Lakers topped the Orlando Magic for their 15th title. And in 2010 they beat the Celtics for Bryant's fifth and final championship ring.
  • (2012) Final Olymic medal - As an aging star at the age of 33, Bryant joined team USA and won his final gold medal at the 2012 summer Olymics in London.
  • (2016) 'Dear Basketball'/Retirement - On Nov. 29, 2015, Bryant published a poem in The Players Tribune, titled "Dear Basketball" where he announced his retirement. On Apr. 13, 2016 he played his final NBA game where he scored a season high 60 points in a win over Utah Jazz.
  • (2017) Jersey retirement - On Dec. 18, 2017, the Lakers retired Bryants jersey numbers both No.8 and No.24. He had worn No.8 until 2006 shen he changed to No.24.
  • (2018) Oscar - Bryant went on to make an animated short film out his "Dear Basketball" poem in April of 2017. the film won the Oscar for Best Animated short Film, Sports Emmy, and the Annie Award of the year at the 90th Academy Awards.
